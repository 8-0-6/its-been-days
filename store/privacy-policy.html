Privacy Policy

It's Been Days Chrome Extension — Last updated: March 2026

It's Been Days is a Chrome extension that helps you track tab inactivity and retrieve closed tabs. Your privacy is the foundation of how this extension is built.

What data is collected and where it's stored

The extension stores the following data locally on your device only, using Chrome's built-in chrome.storage.local API:

  • Tab URLs, titles, and favicon URLs (for tracking and display purposes)
  • Timestamps of when tabs were opened and last visited
  • A running archive of closed tabs (capped at 500 entries, FIFO eviction)
  • Your notification preferences (threshold and enabled/disabled state)

None of this data ever leaves your device. No browsing history, tab data, or usage information is transmitted to any server, ever.

Account and subscription data

If you choose to create an account (required only to subscribe), the following minimal data is stored on our servers (hosted by Supabase):

  • Your email address
  • Your trial start date
  • Your subscription status (trial, active, past_due, or cancelled)
  • A Stripe customer ID (if you subscribe)

An account is not required to use the extension during the free trial. Sign-in is only needed to manage a paid subscription.

Payment processing

Payments are processed by Stripe. We do not store or see your credit card number. Stripe's privacy policy governs their handling of payment information.

Authentication

Sign-in uses magic links sent to your email — no password is stored. Authentication tokens are stored locally in chrome.storage.local and used only to communicate with your own account record.

Third-party services

  • Supabase — authentication and subscription status storage
  • Stripe — payment processing

No analytics, advertising, or tracking services are used. No data is sold or shared with any third party for marketing purposes.

Permissions

The extension requests the following Chrome permissions:

  • tabs — to read tab URLs, titles, and activity status
  • storage — to save tab metadata and settings on your device
  • notifications — to show optional desktop reminders about inactive tabs
  • activeTab — to inject the search overlay into the current tab on keyboard shortcut
  • scripting — to inject the overlay content script
  • alarms — to run the daily inactivity check
  • host_permissions (<all_urls>) — required to inject the overlay content script into any page when the keyboard shortcut is pressed

Data retention and deletion

Local data (tab history, settings) is stored until you clear it via Settings → Archive → Clear, or until you uninstall the extension.

Account data (email, subscription status) is deleted upon request. To request deletion, contact us at the email below.

Children's privacy

This extension is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13.

Changes to this policy

If we make material changes to this privacy policy, we will update the "Last updated" date above. Continued use of the extension after changes constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.
